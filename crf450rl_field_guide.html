Left 14L Packing Order
Sleeping pad first (bottom, heavy). Quilt+sheet in 10L dry sack. Footprint rolled tight. Tent in dry bag on top. Chair tube vertical along bag edge. Stove kit and toiletries fill gaps. Camp shoes on top for easy camp access.
22L Stinger Rule
AirMini hard case inside a 3-4L dry bag liner, centered with clothing as a soft buffer on all sides. ADC MEDIC case upright. EXP48PRO battery accessible without unpacking tent. Never compress the ADC case — rigid and packed tight.
Crash Safety Protocol
AirMini and ADC case stay in 22L Stinger (on bike). Switch 2 and trauma kit in Kriega Trail 9 (on body). Items on body survive a crash with you. Items on bike take the bike impact. Medical kit must be on body — always.

Field Protocols
Bear · Medical · Breakdown · Weather · CPAP failure · Lost
Always-On Rule
Garmin inReach Mini 2 must be powered on, tracking active, and on your body (Kriega Trail 9) for every ride. Emergency contacts must receive your tracking link before departure. Never bury it in a bag.
🐻
Bear Encounter
1Stay calm. Do not run. Running triggers predatory chase response.
2Make yourself large. Raise arms. Speak in firm, low voice. Do not make eye contact.
3Unholster UDAP 15SO. Remove safety. Do not deploy until bear is within 30-40 ft.
4Charging bear: deploy spray in wide Z-pattern at face level. Aim slightly low — spray rises. Hold trigger until bear stops or turns.
5Back away slowly. Do not turn your back. Do not run.
!Physical contact with black bear: FIGHT BACK aggressively. Target eyes and nose. Black bears respond to resistance.
🩹
Medical Emergency
!Severe bleeding: CAT tourniquet 2-3 in above wound. Note time. Pack with Combat Gauze and Israeli bandage.
1Stop. Move to safe position off trail or road.
2Assess: Airway, Breathing, Circulation.
3Life-threatening: activate inReach SOS (hold 3 seconds). Do not accidentally cancel.
4SAM splint for suspected fractures. AMK kit in Right 14L.
5Trauma kit is ON BODY (Kriega pack) — if you crash, the kit is with you, not on the bike.
🔧
Bike Breakdown
1Move bike off trail. Note GPS coordinates on Garmin.
2Flat: plugs + CO2 first. If sidewall: tube replacement. Tools in Right 14L.
3Chain: master link in tool roll. Confirm before every trip.
4Unrideable: text GPS coordinates via inReach. Stay with bike unless life safety requires leaving.
5Overnight wait: Left 14L has full tent and sleep system. Set up camp.
Tool roll contents to confirm every trip: zip ties (T&B TY5275MX), patches, valve cores, master link, tire levers, chain lube.
⛈
Severe Weather
1Lightning: dismount immediately. Distance yourself from bike. Move to low ground away from isolated trees.
2Do NOT shelter under isolated trees, cliff faces, or near water/streams.
3Flash flood (Colorado canyon country): high ground immediately. Never camp in dry washes.
4Hypothermia: wet + wind is the danger. Rain layer in Kriega pack. Get dry shelter immediately.
5NEMO tent: stake ALL guylines, close vestibules. Handles sustained rain without re-tensioning.
Colorado afternoon thunderstorms: plan to be off exposed ridges by noon. Check NWS forecast every morning.
😴
CPAP Failure
1EXP48PRO battery dead: connect Nitecore Summit 20000 via USB-C + 20V PD to AirMini DC cable. EXP48PRO itself runs the AirMini 2-3 nights on a charge (humidifier/heated tube off).
2Mask seal failure: check cushion for debris. HumidX replacement in ADC case.
3Machine error: ResMed support 1-800-424-0737. inReach can relay via satellite SMS if no cell.
!If CPAP unavailable: limit next-day riding distance. Sleep quality directly impacts riding alertness and safety. This is not optional equipment.
📍
Lost / Comms Down
1Stop riding. Do not continue if disoriented. Check GPS coordinates on Garmin zūmo XT3.
2Send coordinates via inReach text to emergency contacts. Works anywhere with sky view.
3Garmin zūmo XT3 has offline maps. Works without cell signal. Offline maps must be downloaded before departure.
4Stay with the bike. Bikes are easier to find than people.
5inReach SOS is always available regardless of cell coverage. Use without hesitation if situation is life-threatening.
